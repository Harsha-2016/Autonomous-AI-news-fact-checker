The Indian Space Research Organisation successfully launched the PSLV-C58 
mission on January 1, 2024, carrying the XPoSat satellite — India's first 
dedicated X-ray polarimetry observatory. The satellite was placed in a 
650km orbit and will study cosmic X-ray sources including black holes and 
neutron stars. ISRO chairman S. Somanath confirmed the mission success from 
the Sriharikota launch centre. XPoSat makes India only the second country 
after the United States to have an operational X-ray polarimetry satellite 
in orbit.
